India One
The Consuming Class

Our Thesis

We invest where India is going,
not where it has been.

India is experiencing a structural shift — from unbranded to branded, from mass to niche, from offline to omnichannel. The brands that win the next decade are being built today, for a very different consumer.

Think9 backs founders who understand this — who see that technology redesigns consumption, legacy categories are ripe for redefinition, and knowing the pulse of India unlocks opportunities no global template can offer.

Investment Pillars

Four pillars of our conviction

01
Secular to Niche
Technology and media redesign how consumers consume. The era of "happiness for everyone" brands gives way to targeted niches — nostalgia (Paper Boat), protein (Epigamia), natural (RAW). We back founders who own their tribe.
02
Legacy Reinvented
The old comes back in new forms. Ayurveda reimagined with clinical validation and D2C (Kapiva, ₹345Cr at 87% CAGR). Commodity ceiling fans redefined with BLDC tech (Atomberg, 23% premium share). Legacy categories are the lowest hanging fruit.
03
The Educated Consumer
Consumption today is a profession. Consumers demand ingredient transparency — exact percentages replace vague claims. Minimalist sold to Unilever for ₹2,955Cr by winning label-reading consumers. We back brands built on this foundation.
04
India Over Global Templates
Tier-2/3 consumers trust WhatsApp resellers over Amazon — Meesho has 175Mn users, 87% non-metro. Gen-Z rejects inherited consumption. Winning brands will be distinctly, deeply Indian.
