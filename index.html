The India Story

India is becoming the
3rd largest economy by 2030.

Growing at 8% annually — nearly 3× the US and Germany — India's private consumption powers 61% of nominal GDP. Beyond $2,500 per capita, discretionary income grows disproportionately, and India is hitting that inflection point right now.

The top 30 million Indian households (India-1) dominate 70–89% of value-added consumption — and this cohort is adding 30 million more households by 2030. A consumer market unlike anything the world has seen.

Our Framework

India One & India Two

India One
The Consuming Class
Those able to afford domestic help. India-1 represents the top 30 million households dominating 70–89% of value-added consumption — beverages, beauty, dining, durables, travel. Adding 30 million more by 2030.
India Two
The Serving Class
The backbone of Indian society — domestic help, drivers, shop-floor workers, delivery personnel. As India-1 grows, so does the aspirational spending of India-2. An entirely new brand cohort waiting to be served.

Staples & FMCG Still Unbranded
01
Secular to Niche
Technology and media redesign how consumers consume. "Happiness for everyone" brands give way to targeted niches — nostalgia (Paper Boat), protein (Epigamia), natural (RAW). We back founders who own their tribe.
02
Legacy Reinvented
The old comes back in new forms. Ayurveda reimagined with clinical validation and D2C (Kapiva, ₹345Cr at 87% CAGR). Commodity ceiling fans redefined with BLDC tech (Atomberg, 23% premium share). Legacy categories are the lowest hanging fruit.
03
The Educated Consumer
Consumption today is a profession. Consumers demand ingredient transparency — exact percentages replace vague claims. Minimalist sold to Unilever for ₹2,955Cr by winning label-reading consumers.
04
India Over Global Templates
Tier-2/3 consumers trust WhatsApp resellers over Amazon — Meesho has 175Mn users, 87% non-metro. Gen-Z rejects inherited consumption. Winning brands will be distinctly, deeply Indian.
